## Wave 29 Spawn — First Actions

Once wave 29 spawns, ask yourself one question:

> **Are there undimmed magers or rangers in the room?**

```
Yes → Hit those crabs, keep hitting relevant crabs as more appear
No  → BP irrelevant (dimmed) crabs to push the waves until relevant crabs spawn
```

> **Skip your wave 29 west flickers** if you have relevant undimmed cleanup splits available

**Where to expect splits consistently:**
- If your mager performed the wave 26 dinhs → expect splits from the **southwest pillar** immediately after wave 29 spawns
- Shortly after → expect splits from the **wave 23 ranger big** on the **northeast pillar** *(unless your mager popped it with a 24 dinhs)*

## Wave 26 Big (After Turning Range)

> Only pop this if you have **no relevant splits and no irrelevant rangers** left

Popping it while relevant splits or irrelevant rangers are still available will often chain stall waves 29 and 30:
- An entire wave's worth of relevant crabs is far more efficient than 2 random splits
- Waves 30 and 31 are guaranteed to keep you hitting range crabs — random splits are exactly that, random

## Wave 31 North Mages — Mage vs Range

Use the formula below to decide whether to kill the wave 31 mages as **mage or range**:

```
Wave 31 spawn + 15.6 = x

x < your cleanup target  → kill as RANGE
x ≥ your cleanup target  → kill as MAGE
```

**Examples** *(assuming 233 wave 29 spawn → 304.8 cleanup target)*:
```
Wave 31 spawns at 250 → 250 + 15.6 = 305.6 → more than 304.8 → kill as RANGE
Wave 31 spawns at 245 → 245 + 15.6 = 300.6 → less than 304.8 → kill as MAGE
```

**Reference Table** *(29 spawn → cleanup target → boss spawn)*
```
248 → 319.2 → 328.8
245 → 316.8 → 326.4
243 → 314.4 → 324.0
241 → 312.0 → 321.6
238 → 309.6 → 319.2
236 → 307.2 → 316.8
233 → 304.8 → 314.4
231 → 302.4 → 312.0
228 → 300.0 → 309.6
226 → 257.6 → 307.2
224 → 255.2 → 304.8
221 → 252.8 → 302.4
```

> Drop decimals when doing this in real time — after a while you'll find yourself just comparing two numbers instinctively

## General Cleanup Notes

**Chin usage:**
- Throw chins south and don't be afraid to null everything
- It is the **mager's job** to clean south before going north
- It is **your job** to clean north after clearing all rangers in the room

**Positioning awareness:**
- Pay attention to where you and your mager both are at all times
- If you're on the same side of the room, make sure your hits are deliberate and based on proximity
- Every decision during cleanup should be **intentional and well thought out**
