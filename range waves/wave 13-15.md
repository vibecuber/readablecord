__**Waves 13-15**__

---
## Wave 13 Spawn — Without 12 Dinhs

- Always swap to **blowpipe after scything wave 12**
- **Always BP → Ayak**, never Ayak → BP for waves 13, 14, and 15 if you are not going to lose ticks by piping first
> BP first is more tick efficient to play off of

---
## Wave 13 Spawn — With 12 Dinhs

Assuming you aren't stuck on rangers when wave 13 spawns *(rangers always take priority)*:

- Swap to ayak and be ready to **1-tick any mages** that spawn from the wave 11 big *(the mager will null these)*
- If wave 13 spawns before the splits arrive → ayak that instead

> Either way, be ready to ayak **one of these two options** the moment it's available

---
## Wave 14 Spawn — With 12 Dinhs

Depending on what splits are available before wave 14 spawns, you will open either with an ayak or a bp.

> Recognizing when you can **BP first then ayak** will maximize ticks per wave check and minimize stalls

---
## Wave 15 Spawn — With 12 Dinhs

```
Ended wave 14 on ayak → start wave 15 with BP
Ended wave 14 on BP   → start wave 15 with ayak
```
> This assumes no range splits are available to play off of between the south and east lane

---
## 14 Range Big — When to Pop It

Pop the wave 14 range big somewhere between **waves 15-17** — this is not a fixed time and requires room state observation.

**The rule:**
> Pop the 14 range big when there are **3 smalls** from waves 12-15 still alive

- Count the crabs or simply recognize that more than 3 smalls with wave 12-15 labels are alive
- This staggers both bigs to split at optimal times, whether before or after wave 18 spawns

> This still depends on how well you clear through your wave 16 rangers, but applying this rule will avoid stalls in this section of the waves very consistently

**Building the habit:**
Paying close attention to waves 12-15 makes the count easier in the moment — eventually it becomes second nature and you won't need to actively think about it
