__**Nylo Stalls — Understanding Normality**__

This channel is a guide to understanding normality in the waves. Knowing the difference between a **normal stall**, an **RNG stall**, and an **accidental stall** is key to improving your Nylos.

## __Pre-Cap__

**Normal Stalls**
- There are **zero** intentional stalls in pre-cap

**RNG Stalls**
- Stalling between waves **10-16** is normal to an extent
- Averaging **1-2 stalls** per pre-cap is normal, with 0 being relatively common and 4 or more being outliers
- On very rare occasions, wave **6** and wave **17** can stall without a mistake

**Accidental Stalls**
- Any stall **before wave 10** is almost always a mistake
- A **wave 17** stall is almost always a mistake
- **Wave 18 and 19** stalls are always a mistake

---
## __Post-Cap__

**Normal Stalls**
- It is **intentional** to stall wave 28 three times *(can extend to 3 wave 29 stalls on insta 29)*
- Stalling wave 29 is normal, but the number of stalls should reflect how many bigs you popped:
```
1-2 bigs popped → ~1-2 stalls after wave 29 spawns
3 bigs popped   → more stalls expected
4 bigs popped   → even more stalls expected
```
> There is a ratio where **(stalls : bigs killed)** is either a net positive or negative. Too many stalls relative to bigs killed means you likely popped too much. Too few wave 29-30 stalls likely means you didn't pop enough. Channel links below will explain why this matters and how to execute it correctly.

**RNG Stalls**
- Stalling waves **20-27** due to RNG is rare but does occasionally happen
- Stalling wave **28 more than 3 times** without a mistake is rare but does occur

**Accidental Stalls**
- A **wave 20-27** stall is almost always a reflection of mistakes
- **More than 3 wave 28 stalls** is a mistake unless you aren't going for max efficiency
- **Too many wave 29-30 stalls** is a sign of a mistake
