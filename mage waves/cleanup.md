__**Waves 29-31 Cleanup**__

---
## Wave 29 Spawn — First Actions

On wave 29 spawn, prioritize in this order:

**If there are fewer than 4 splits incoming:**
- Take the **26/28 barrage** or any barrage of equal value
- If no equal barrage is available → scythe the **27 big** or a **small melee** under the 25 or 27
> This ensures you have splits in case wave 30 doesn't spawn quickly after 29

---
## Managing Bigs After Wave 29

> :warning: Do **not** default to popping a big after wave 29 spawns if the room state is bad — too many 29/30 stalls will hurt both you and your ranger by forcing early ayak usage and killing barrage potential

- If the room state is bad → hit **s27s or higher** instead of bigs to reach waves 30-31 faster

**If 30-31 is slow to spawn relative to how many bigs you killed 26-29, consider whether you:**
```
→ Prepped too many splits appearing after wave 29 spawned
→ Popped too many bigs on wave 28
→ Weren't focused on s27 or higher splits after wave 26
```

---
## Wave 30 Spawn

- If **29 and 30 spawn without a stall** and you can react in time → ayak 30 **before** wave 29 reaches melee distance
- Let **30 melees turn mage** if that means you can barrage them instead — otherwise just melee them

From here, run **normal north/south cleanup** until wave 31 spawns:
- Prioritize **melee gear**
- Use a **1-tick 2-way switch** to react to mages if no melees are close by

---
## Wave 31 Mage Small

Always barrage this if possible. If you can't barrage, use the formula below to decide whether to kill it as **mage** or **range**.

---
**The Math**

> Your wave 29 spawn time = your expiry reference point
```
Wave 31 spawn + 15.6 = x

x < your cleanup target  → kill as RANGE
x ≥ your cleanup target  → kill as MAGE
```

*Example: wave 31 spawns at 243, cleanup target is 304.8*
`243 + 15.6 = 258.6 → less than 304.8 → kill as range`

---
