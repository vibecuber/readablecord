__**Waves 8-13**__

This channel covers advanced strategies to reduce stalls between waves 8-13, starting immediately after you kill the wave 8 mage big.

---
## After the Wave 8 Big — Two Immediate Priorities

**Priority 1 — Clear mage smalls**
Stay in mage gear and kill ~2 mage smalls before wave 10 spawns.
> When wave 10 spawns, avoid maging unless the target is in melee distance — it likely won't register before the wave 11 check.

**Priority 2 — Clear the east side of the SW pillar**
Kill any small landing on the eastward-facing side of the southwest pillar.
> This is what sets up your wave 12 dinhs correctly so you don't accidentally hit the big.
> You may need to watch for this across waves 10, 11, and 12.

## Wave 11 Spawn

**Try to be first tick:**
- 1st tick is ideal — it will clear in time before the wave 12 check
- 2nd tick still clears in time if you hug the barrier

- :x: **Don't:** Barrage east → Ayak 11 big → Barrage south when the room is full
  - Stalling wave 12 here will cause a chain stall
- :white_check_mark: **Do:** Barrage east → Barrage south → Ayak 11
- :white_check_mark: **If the room is full:** Barrage east → Mac → Barrage south *(maximizes your chances of not stalling 11)*

> **General safety rule:** Don't ayak the 11 big until wave 12 has spawned.

**Smalls vs. big — read the room:**
If your ranger has plenty of time to kill the 11 big, you can prioritize smalls instead. Prioritize in this order:
```
1. East melees
2. SW pillar eastward-facing mages/melees
3. Mages in general
```
> This is a **room state decision** — you won't make the same call every time.

## Wave 12 Dinhs

**Scything the east wave 12 melee doubles:**
- Click the **south** melee
  - Your big scythe hit lands on the one that doesn't follow you
  - The north melee *(which does follow you)* takes the small hit and becomes part of the dinhs
- After scything, **path diagonally north** before clicking the wave 12 aggro on the west side
  - This avoids accidentally popping the wave 12 big when the room is full
  - If the room isn't full, you can skip this movement and pop the big freely

**Timing the scythe:**
- Scythe on the **first tick** they're in melee range
  - A tick late means the following dinhs won't register before the wave 14 check
- If something forces you a tick late *(e.g. you had to hit something beforehand)*:
  - **Mac the south crab** instead of scything — this re-syncs you for an on-tick dinhs

## After the Wave 12 Dinhs

**Hit one of:**
- South wave 13 melee, **or**
- West wave 14 mage

**Priority notes:**
- Wave 12 melees and south wave 13 melees are **lowest value** — they expire between waves 18-19 on a zero stall pre-cap
- The west wave 13 mage aggro is a fine hit **before wave 14 spawns**, but don't prioritize it over 14s or 15s
  - Leaving 14s or 15s alive increases the risk of wave 18 stalls
- **Ignore "s14" mage splits** — these are nulled if both players executed the wave 12 dinhs correctly
  - Your ranger can clean these up if needed
  - If you notice they weren't nulled, feel free to hit them yourself

---
## When to Pop the 13 Melee Big

The rule is straightforward:

> **Scythe the 13 melee big when there are 2 smalls alive from waves 12-15**

Sometimes the big will split before 18 spawns, and sometimes after — this is normal variation. **Don't rush it,** and **always** pay attention to the room state to make this decision.
