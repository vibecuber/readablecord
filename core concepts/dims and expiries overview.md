__**What Are Dims & Expiries?**__

---
## Why Should I Dim?

Dims help players identify **which crabs not to hit** when it isn't immediately obvious. They give you a rough real-time indicator of when a crab will expire.

> At certain moments in the waves, a dim is more of a **suggestion** than a hard rule for experienced players — the optimal play is sometimes to hit a dimmed crab. This is covered throughout the guide.

## What Are the Dim Options?

There are four dims, referred to in shorthand by the fastest possible time achievable with each:

```
302 dim  (aka 28-12 dim  / 2-stall 28 dim)
304 dim  (aka 28-16 dim  / 3-stall 28 dim)
307 dim  (aka 28-20 dim  / 4-stall 28 dim)
309 dim  (aka 28-24 dim  / 5-stall 28 dim)
```

---
## Which Dim Should I Use?

**By current average:**
```
319 or worse  → 309 dim
~316          → 307 dim
~314          → 307 dim
~312          → 304 dim
~309          → 304 dim (or 302 if you want a challenge)
```

**By feel:**
- Consistently killing your last undimmed crab a **full cycle before everything else expires** → move to a harder dim
- Rarely killing your last undimmed crab before everything expires → move to an easier dim
- Frequently hitting **319 or worse** on a harder dim → drop to an easier dim

---
**General Recommendations:**

```
+------------------+-----------------+
| Experience Level | Recommended Dim |
+------------------+-----------------+
| Learner          | 309 dim         |
+------------------+-----------------+
| Average          | 307 dim         |
+------------------+-----------------+
| Experienced      | 304 dim         |
+------------------+-----------------+
| Chasing 302      | 302 dim         |
+------------------+-----------------+
```

> The difference between 302 dim and 304 dim is small — a 302 is technically achievable on the 304 dim. Don't jump to 302 dim unless you're specifically targeting a 302 time.

> Don't be afraid to experiment with multiple dims to find what gives you the best averages at your current stage. Jumping straight to 302 or 304 dim can harm your averages, learning experience, and motivation.
